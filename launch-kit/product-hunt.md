## Full description

Every notes app eventually holds your second brain hostage: a proprietary format, a monthly bill, an export button you pray works. Quillpad refuses all of it.

Point Quillpad at any folder. Every note is a real markdown file, every folder is a real folder. Edit them in VS Code, sync them with Dropbox or git, grep them from a terminal — Quillpad watches the disk and stays in sync.

On top of those plain files you get the connected-notes features people pay subscriptions for:

- **[[Wikilinks]]** with fuzzy autocomplete — click to follow, auto-creates missing notes
- **Backlinks panel** showing every note that links here, with context
- **#tags** with a clickable tag sidebar and filtering
- **Full-text search** across the entire vault, matches highlighted
- **Daily notes** (Ctrl+D) and a **global quick-capture** popup (Ctrl+Shift+N from anywhere in Windows → inbox.md)
- **Ctrl+P quick switcher**, recent files first
- Live-styled CodeMirror 6 editor with full preview toggle

100% local. Zero network calls. MIT-licensed source on GitHub — the $29 gets you the polished one-click installer and my eternal gratitude.

Pay once. Own it forever. Your notes were always yours; now your notes app is too.

## Maker first comment

Hey PH 👋

I built Quillpad because I did the math on my own "second brain": Notion wanted $144/year, Evernote $180/year, and even Obsidian — which I love for its plain files — charges monthly for the sync/convenience layer. Ten years of note-taking at subscription prices is a used car.

Meanwhile my actual requirement was embarrassingly simple: markdown files in a folder, linked together, searchable, with a quick way to capture thoughts. That's a $29 problem, not a $15/month problem.

So Quillpad is exactly that. No account. No cloud. If I get hit by a bus, your notes are still a folder of .md files that open in anything. The indexer that powers wikilinks/backlinks/tags is a pure, unit-tested Node module and the whole thing is MIT on GitHub.

Ask me anything — especially "why not just use X", I have opinions 🪶
